The purpose of these charts is to answer the question of "Does a smaller population mean more people get vaccinated?" The motivation behind this chart was the idea that smaller groups of people tend to think the same, while larger groups are more likely to think differently from each other. The total of vaccinations are the total number of COVID-19 vaccination doses administered per 100 people in the total population. In the chart below, the green shaded bubbles represent different countries. When hovered over, the total population and the total vaccinations per hundred are shown.
